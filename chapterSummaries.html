Chapter Summaries

  • Prologue
  • The Chorus appears on stage and deliver a sonnet setting up the play
  • The sonnet describes two rival houses who will both produce a person who will fall in love with the other causing an even greater schism between the houses
  • There love will eventually result in each others deaths
  • Act 1 Scene 1
  • Two servants, Sampson and Gregory, who work in the house of Capulet, walk down the streets in Verona, Sampson is complaining of his hatred for the Montague house
  • They joke about taking over the house, conquering the men, and sexually taking over the women, as they joke they see two men that work at the Montague house, and want to provoke a fight
  • Sampson decides to bite his thumb at the Montagues which is insulting, and after a verbal altercation a fight ensues
  • A Montague kinsman, Benvolio, steps in and tries to stop the fight by drawing the sword, however, Tybalt, a Capulet kinsmen sees this and draws his sword as well
  • Benvolio says he just wants to keep peace but Tybalt attacks, and a huge brawl ensues resulting in a group of civilians joining as well bringing clubs and attacking the combatants
  • Finally Montague and Capulet show up and both nearly come to blows before there wives stop them
  • Prince Escalus arrives and forces the brawl to end, he then states that there is too much hatred among the two families and that it must stop, there is a death penalty to anyone in either family who disturbs the peace again
  • Capulet and the brawlers leave, leaving just Benvolio and his aunt and uncle, Montague and Lady Montague on stage
  • Benvolio tells of the fight, and Lady Montague changes the topic asking if he's seen her son, Romeo, who was last seen pacing through a grove of sycamores outside the city
  • The Montague's are worried about there son who has seemed rather melancholy lately, Benvolio decides he will approach him and see what's bothering him
  • Romeo says that he is in love with Rosaline, yet she doesn't have the same feelings, and has vowed her life to chastity which leaves Romeo heart broken
  • He says nothing can change feeling but Benvolio must resolve to help him
  • Act 1 Scene 2
  • On a different street in Verona, Capulet and Paris, a kinsmen of the Prince, walk and discus Paris' desire to wed one of Capulet's daughter, Juliet
  • Capulet is thrilled, but states that Juliet, who is 14, is too young to marry and Paris must wait two years
  • Capulet tells Paris that he is his preferred suitor so there is nothing to worry about, and then invites Paris to a feast that night where he can begin his wooing of Juliet
  • Capulet then sends a servant, Peter, to invite a list of people
  • Peter can not read and laments about the difficult task ahead
  • Peter walks past Romeo and Benvolio and asks for there help reading the list, sure enough Rosaline's name is on the list
  • Peter invites Romeo and Benvolio to the feast, he assumes they are not Montagues, and the men agree as Benvolio believes it will be nice for Romeo to meet new women, yet Romeo just wants to see Rosaline
  • Act 1 Scene 3
  • In the Capulet house before the feast begins Lady Capulet speaks to her daughter Juliet, her nurse is also present providing advice as well
  • The nurse begins telling a story about how as a child Juliet was unaware she was part of a sexual joke, both Juliet and her mother are not amused and force her to quit speaking
  • The mother and daughter then speak of marriage, as Lady Capulet explains that she had Juliet when she was about Juliet's age
  • She tells Juliet of Paris' desire to marry her, and Juliet says she will look upon him and see if she might love him
  • The feast is about to begin
  • Act 1 Scene 4
  • The feast is a masquerade, where everyone wears masks, and Romeo, Benvolio, and Mercutio, there friend, all wear masks on there way to the feast
  • Romeo is worried about dancing because he is a Montague and would not want to give away his identity, the other men mock him, and give him a hard time about his talk of love as they joke using sexual metaphors
  • Romeo is annoyed with the metaphors, he states he had a dream that the feast was a bad decision
  • Mercutio then gives a long speech about Queen Mab of the fairies, she visits people in dreams, Mercutio becomes seemingly entranced in his speech and Romeo is forced to calm the man
  • Mercutio then says he was speaking about nothing and dreams are childish
  • Benvolio settles the men so they can plan on how to actually get in the feast
  • Romeo states that be believes attending the feast will set in motion a fate for himself that will ultimately lead to his death
  • But he puts his faith in the hands of god who has control of his future, relaxes and continues on to the feast
  • Act 1 Scene 5
  • At the Capulet house there is a mad scramble among servants to appease the guests and Capulet paces around greeting guests, joking with them, and encouraging dancing
  • Romeo sees across the room, Juliet, and is awestruck
  • He asks who she is but the serving-man he asks is of no help, Romeo believes he is finally in love for the first time, Rosaline is forgotten almost instantly
  • Tybalt overhears Romeo's voice and at once recognizes him as a Montague, he goes to fetch his sword but is stopped by Capulet who states that Romeo is well respected and he shall not be harmed at the feast
  • Tybalt protests but ultimately agrees, he does however vow to not forget that Romeo broke this unwritten rule
  • Romeo makes his way to Juliet, touches her hand and begins to speak to her
  • He uses many religious metaphors to state that he is a pilgrim who hopes to resolve his sin, and Juliet, a saint, can only do that through a kiss
  • She agrees to allow this, but then states his sins are now residing in her lips, so they must kiss again
  • Following the kiss the Nurse arrives calling away Juliet to speak to her mother
  • Romeo asks the nurse who Juliet is and learns she is the daughter of Capulet which devastates him
  • Romeo is then lead away by Benvolio, while Juliet is elsewhere contemplating the encounter
  • She is infatuated with Romeo, and states that if he is married she will die, she employs the nurse to discover her lovers identity
  • She learns that it is Romeo, a Montague, which leaves her with anguish as a love between a Capulet and Montague is forbidden, she then leaves the hall with the nurse
  • Act 2 Prologue
  • The Chorus returns delivering another sonnet
  • This sonnet tells of the new found love, and how the lovers will do whatever it takes to overcome the obstacles in there way to love one another
  • Act 2 Scene 1
  • Romeo decides he can not leave before finding Juliet so he decides to scale a wall bordering the Capulet property and enter the orchard to look for her
  • Mercutio and Benvolio search for Romeo but can not find him as he does not want to be found
  • Mercutio then begins to mock Romeo's feelings for Rosaline allowed, before exiting with Benvolio
  • Romeo did over hear him and speaks to himself questioning how Mercutio can mock him when he himself has not felt the pain of love
  • Romeo then comes across Juliet who is standing above him in a window
  • He resolves to not startle her by speaking, and he overhears her stating her love for him, stating that if he were to give up the Montague name she would declare her love, and vise versa
  • Romeo overhears this and decides to speak stating his love for her, she is shocked to see him, wondering how he found her and he states that it was love that brought him there
  • She is worried that he will be caught, and at the same time worried they are moving too fast, but she does state again that she loves him
  • Juliet is called away by her nurse but returns briefly to set up a meeting, and after one last time confessing there love, they go there separate ways
  • Romeo departs in search of a Monk that can help him with his cause
  • Act 2 Scene 2
  • On stage it is early morning and Friar Lawrence enters holding a basket with herbs, weeds, and flowers, which he has a deep knowledge of
  • Romeo arrives and Friar Lawrence is a little bit shocked as it appears as if Romeo has not slept at all
  • He believes Romeo has slept with Rosaline which would be a great sin, however Romeo assures him that this is false
  • Romeo tells of his new love leaving the friar shocked again, as he says it seems rather fickle
  • Romeo defends himself explaining of his deep love for her, and the friar does comment that the love between Romeo and Rosaline did not seem quite right, and that perhaps Juliet might suit him better
  • Romeo asks if Friar Lawrence will marry the two, and he agrees, seeing that it might help bring a end to the feud between families
  • Act 2 Scene 3
  • Meanwhile Mercutio and Benvolio wonder what happened to Romeo as he did not return home, Mercutio then begins to bad mouth Rosaline again
  • Benvolio then states that Tybalt has sent a letter to Romeo challenging to him a dual, Mercutio responds saying Romeo is already dead at the hands of Cupid's arrow
  • He wonders to himself whether Romeo would be willing to dual and strong enough to defeat Tybalt, and Benvolio defends Romeo
  • Mercutio then goes into deep detail about how strong and talented a swordsman Tybalt is, which would make it very difficult for Romeo
  • Mercutio then adds that he despises Tybalt and all he stands for
  • Romeo finally arrives and is greeted by criticism from Mercutio who believes Romeo is still wrought with love for Rosaline, and that accounts for his disappearance
  • Romeo denies this and says he left because of a very important matter
  • The men begin to proceed with witty sexual jokes among one another
  • As the men joke, the Nurse and Peter arrive searching for Romeo, who presents himself
  • Mercutio makes fun of the Nurse calling her a harlot, and upsetting her
  • Mercutio and Benvolio leave to dinner and Romeo stays behind to speak to the Nurse, she warns him to not play games with Juliet's heart, and she assures he would do no such thing
  • He tells her to inform Juliet that they should meet at Friar Lawrence's place where they will be wed, and The Nurse agrees, mentioning she will place a cloth ladder in Juliet's room so that Romeo may enter on there wedding night
  • Act 2 Scene 4
  • Juliet waits for the Nurse's return and to learn what Romeo said
  • At first she is reluctant to tell Juliet, stating she is too tired, but ultimately gives in telling her Romeo is in wait for her to meet them so they can marry
  • The Nurse leaves to wait for Romeo's servant who will provide the cloth ladder
  • Act 2 Scene 5
  • Romeo waits with Father Lawrence for Juliet to arrive, he is ecstatic and does not care as to what misfortune could become of there marriage
  • The Friar warns Romeo that he should be wise about his love as often time there are terrible endings to those who are to passionate
  • Juliet arrives and Romeo asks her to speak poetically of her love yet she states that her love is too powerful to describe with word
  • Act 3 Scene 1
  • Benvolio suggests to Mercutio while waking in the street if they should avoid a brawl with the Capulet men by heading inside
  • When Tybalt enters, Mercutio makes fun of him when he gets annoyed
  • Once Romeo enters, Tybalt turns to him and calls him a villain
  • He considers him a villain because he is secretly married to Juliet which makes Romeo Tybalt's kinsman
  • Tybalt declares that he wants to fight with Romeo
  • When Romeo refuses, Tybalt insists until he realizes Romeo's love for him and that he will not fight him
  • When this happens Mercutio decides to fight him.
  • Mercutio dies during the fight even though Romeo tries to stop it from happening.
  • When Mercutio dies, Romeo is upset because he'd still be alive had Romeo fought Tybalt.
  • Tybalt returns to the scene and Romeo kills him after a duel
  • Romeo then runs away
  • The Prince enters the stage and Benvolio describes what has happened making sure to mention that Romeo tried to keep things peaceful.
  • Lady Capulet insists that Benvolio is lying to protect the Montagues.
  • After Lady Capulet wants Romeo dead, Prince Escalus exiles him from Verona instead.
  • Act 3 scene 2
  • Juliet waits for darkness so that Romeo can come and see her.
  • While she waits the nurse enters and tells Juliet the story of Romeo and Tybalt's fight making it sound as if Romeo is dead.
  • Finally she knows that Romeo has been exiled and only Tybalt is dead.
  • She then tells the nurse to give Romeo Juliet's ring as a memento of her love and the nurse assures Juliet that Romeo will be at their wedding night
  • Act 3 Scene 3
  • While in his cell, Friar Lawrence tells Romeo that he is lucky he was only banished which
  • Romeo feels that being banished is worse since he cannot see his Juliet
  • The Nurse enters and Romeo begs for good news thinking that Juliet will think of him as a murderer.
  • Friar Lawrence keeps Romeo from stabbing himself
  • He explains that since he and Juliet are still alive that maybe the Prince will allow Romeo to come back after things have died down.
  • Romeo decides to visit Juliet that night and that he will live in Mantua until their marriage can be well known.
  • The Nurse gives Romeo the ring and says good-bye to the Friar
  • Act 3 Scene 4
  • Capulet, Lady Capulet, and Paris converse about Juliet
  • Capulet decides that his word is final and that Paris and Juliet will be married on Wednesday
  • After realizing that it is only Monday he decides that the wedding will be on Thursday
  • Act 3 Scene 5
  • Before morning arrives, Romeo escapes out of Juliet's window in order to avoid being killed
  • Juliet tries to get Romeo to stay by convincing him that it is really night and not morning
  • When Romeo decides to stay he has to leave quickly because it is actually morning and Lady Capulet, Juliet's mom is on her way
  • They both look pale like dead bodies, which Romeo explains to be sorrow.
  • Romeo promises that they will be together again shortly
  • When Juliet's mother enters she mistakes Juliet's tears as grief over Tybalt's death not Romeo's, Juliet even convinces Lady Capulet that she wants Romeo dead too, when she really does not
  • Lady Capulet announces that she will marry Paris on Thursday and Juliet, outraged, demands that she will not
  • This angers Capulet when he enters the stage and threatens to disown Juliet if she does not marry Paris
  • The Nurse tries to get Juliet to marry Paris because she believes that Paris is a much better fit for Juliet since Romeo will most likely be killed
  • To avoid confrontation she pretends that the Nurse is right and that she will go confess to Friar Lawrence
  • She states that if Friar Lawrence is unable to help her then she will kill her self
  • Act 4 Scene 1
  • Friar Lawrence speaks to Paris about his impending marriage to Juliet
  • Paris says Juliet is very upset about Tybalt's death and that her father, Capulet, would like Paris and her to marry soon so as to put an end to her sadness
  • The friar thinks to himself, he would rather not know the reason why Paris and Juliet's marriage should be put on hold
  • Juliet appears on stage and Paris speaks to her very passionately while she uses an indifferent tone in return, she also mentions that the two aren't married yet
  • Juliet then asks to have confession with the friar and Paris leaves
  • Juliet tells Friar that she would rather kill herself than marry Paris
  • Friar has a plan, he will give Juliet sleeping potion the night before the wedding, everyone will think she is dead and she will be put in the Capulet tomb where Romeo will receive her and take her back to Mantua where they can be free
  • Juliet agrees to this plan
  • Act 4 Scene 2
  • Juliet returns home to her parents who are preparing for the wedding
  • She pretends to be thrilled for the marriage and her parents are extremely pleased, even moving the wedding up a day
  • Capulet, her father, leaves to tell Paris
  • Act 4 Scene 3
  • Juliet asks to be left alone that night and her nurse and mother oblige
  • Juliet contemplates the task at hand, what if the potion kills her, what if Romeo doesn't find her
  • Just before taking the potion she has a vision of Tybalt's ghost who searches for Romeo, she begs him to leave the search
  • She then takes a drink from the vial
  • Act 4 Scene 4 and 5
  • The next morning the final preparations are being made for the wedding
  • The Nurse goes to wake Juliet and finds her dead, this sends the whole house into hysterics
  • Paris arrives soon after with Father Lawrence and learns of the news leaving him devastated as well
  • The Friar reminds everyone she is in a better place, and has them begin preparations for the funeral
  • The musicians are left behind in the house and Peter approaches them asking them to play a happy tune to cheer him up
  • They are appalled by the though of happy music and refuse
  • Peter storms out and the musicians wait around in hopes to have food when the mourners return to the house
  • Act 5 Scene 1
  • It is Wednesday in Mantua and Romeo is in a wonderful mood as he just had a dream that he had died and Juliet had revived him with a kiss
  • As he is recalling this Balthasar enters and Romeo expects news of Juliet
  • He is however told of her death which breaks his heart
  • He plans to leave to Verona and gives a letter with Balthasar to give to Montague
  • Balthasar is afraid to leave Romeo but Romeo insists, lastly Romeo asks if there is a letter for him from Friar Lawrence, however there is not
  • Romeo plans to go to the Capulet tomb and lay with Juliet that night before killing himself
  • He goes to the Apothecary that day and requests a poison, the Apothecary first denies him but he convinces them to sell it anyways and Romeo vows to use it that night once with Juliet
  • Act 5 Scene 2
  • Friar Lawrence and Friar John speak, we learn that Lawrence had given John a letter to deliver to Romeo explaining Juliet was still alive
  • John however says he could not deliver it as he was quarantined do to a plague out break
  • Lawrence is worried because Romeo wont know of the plan and no one will be able to retrieve Juliet, so he plans to retrieve her himself and he sends a letter to Romeo to inform him of what happened
  • Act 5 Scene 3
  • That night Paris is placing flowers on Juliet's grave when his servant warns him people are approaching
  • Paris disappears into the dark and Romeo and Balthasar approach wielding a crow-bar
  • Romeo tells Balthasar that he is there to receive a ring he had given to Juliet and that Balthasar should leave and give the letter Romeo wrote to Montague the next day
  • Paris recognizes Romeo and assumes that Romeo is taking revenge on the Capulets to dis-honor the corpse of either Juliet or Tybalt
  • Romeo and Paris dual resulting in Romeo being victorious, Paris is killed and in his dying breaths asks to be placed next to Juliet
  • Romeo agrees and takes his body down to her tomb
  • He sees Juliet and remarks that she looks so beautiful you wouldn't know she was dead
  • He plans to spend eternity with Juliet and takes the potion, taking his life
  • Friar Lawrence enters the churchyard where he sees Balthasar, Balthasar's remarks on how he had a vision that Romeo killed someone in a dual
  • Lawrence is troubled by this statement and heads into the tomb where he finds Paris and Romeo's bodies
  • Juliet awakens and asks the Friar where her husband is, the Friar remarks that both him and Paris are dead, he then tells Juliet she must leave with him now, but he does not wait around for her as he fears being seen in the tomb
  • Juliet goes to Romeo and hopes to kill herself by getting poison off of his lips with a kiss, this does not work so she takes Romeo's dagger and stabs herself, dying on Romeo's chest
  • Chaos ensues outside in the churchyard
  • The Friar and Balthasar are caught outside the tomb, and the Prince and the Capulets arrive and are informed of Romeo, Juliet, and Paris' deaths
  • Montague arrives as well and states that his wife has died out of grief for Romeo's exile
  • Friar tells the story of the secret marriage, and Romeo's letter that is now with Balthasar backs it up
  • The Prince blames the Montagues and Capulets and there feud for the deaths, the two sides finally make peace and both construct a gold statue of Romeo and Juliet besides each other
  • Everyone leaves the stage as they pronounce there has never been a story so heartbreaking as this one
