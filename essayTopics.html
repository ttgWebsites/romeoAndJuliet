Essay Topics

  • How does the short time frame of the play play a role? And is this time frame even plausible?
  • The romance of Romeo and Juliet spans only four short days before the tragic end, however the events that occur during this short time period would suggest a much longer time frame.
  • Being that these events are crammed into such a short time frame it seems to add a sense of heightened drama as every brief moment is essential to the plot.
  • The events also seem to be occurring so quickly that the characters do not have much time to react, and instead must go with the flow.
  • This accelerated plot and romance also seems to play a role in the tragic end, as the characters must react so quickly that the results of their actions are out of their control.
  • The pace of the play does not seem plausible as it seems as if a romance so intense as Romeo and Juliet's would require much more time to develop, however it is just that, a play, and therefore real life is thrown aside for the drama of the stage.
  • Compare and contrast the two 'star-crossed lovers' and how does their personalities play a role in their deep love for one another?
  • Romeo as a character is a very passionate, intelligent, and excitable young man.
  • He is very well liked because he is loyal and a respectable person, he is also noted for his spontaneity.
  • When he discover's Juliet he is awestruck and immediately falls in love.
  • His personality and extreme passion causes him to throw all of himself into the pursuit of his love.
  • Meanwhile Juliet begins the play as a very innocent girl.
  • She too is overwhelmed by a powerful love for Romeo, however hers is not as easily explainable as she has no experience with love, yet Romeo has an overwhelming effect on her.
  • The new found love quickly causes Juliet to mature, and she proves that her feelings for Romeo are not an infatuation but true love.
  • It seems that both of their personalities provide the perfect recipe for a powerful and captivating love, however it is a love that cannot quite be explained, nor should it be as it is so natural and undying.
